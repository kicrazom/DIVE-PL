An AI tool-chain for building survey studies — made reproducible

Łukasz Minarowski, MD, PhD ORCID · Jakub Matuk, BEng ORCID · Maksymilian Lech
Department of Respiratory Physiopathology, Medical University of Białystok, Poland

DIVE-PL is a human-led, AI-assisted methodology: investigators design and orchestrate the study, while a tool-chain (Claude / Cowork, GPT-5.5, REDCap, Python/React dashboards) executes and reviews under human oversight — wrapped in an open-science layer that makes every step verifiable.

Zenodo DOI GitHub repository Docs: CC BY 4.0 Code: MIT

Human-in-the-loop boundary. Every AI output is reviewed and approved by a domain expert before it affects the study. AI augments; it does not replace expert judgment, and AI tools are not co-authors. See Limitations.

The contribution

The novelty is the integration, not any single technique. Each component has prior art; to our knowledge the combination of all four components plus a full open-science reproducibility layer has not been published as a single survey-study methodology (literature scan, mid-2026). See Related work.

Who does what

Investigators (authors)

Humans design and orchestrate the study — research questions, protocol, item content, decisions and interpretation.

GPT-5.5

Independent reviewer (LLM-as-judge) of survey items — flags bias, ambiguity, content-validity issues; fixes loop back to the investigators.

Claude / Cowork

Agentic assistant that builds and operates the REDCap instrument via MCP / browser automation, under investigator direction.

REDCap + dashboards

De-identified electronic data capture (consent / RODO) and AI-assisted Python / React analytics, published openly.

Case study

The framework is demonstrated on a cross-sectional online survey of recreational divers in Poland (health, fitness via a non-exercise VO2max proxy, safety knowledge, and self-reported adverse events). The study is ethics-approved and registered (protocol APK.002.228.2026). No participant data are published here.
